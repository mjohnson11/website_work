Overview

Populations of yeast with diverse random DNA barcodes were evolved in different environments (corresponding to the colored buttons below), clones were selected from each environment, and the fitness (relative to an ancestor) of these clones was tested in several environments. The graph below is a representation of the "joint distribution of fitnesses" between two environments. Each point represents a single clone, and its position represents its fitness (where 1 is the fitness of the ancestor) in each of two test environments. If you click on the buttons on either axis of the graph you can change that axis to display the fitness in another test environment. At right, a "spaghetti plot" shows the fitness values of each clone as a line, where the height corresponds to its fitness in each environment. By sequencing the barcode region at several timepoints during 5-day all-vs-all competitions in each of the test environments, we measured the changes in frequency of each clone, and calculated its fitness using those trajectories. You can see these raw trajectories at the bottom of the page. If you hover over a lineage, you can see its barcode information in addition to its evolution or "home" environment. If you click on one of the colored buttons below you can highlight clones from only one of these home environments. If barcode lineage tracking data is available from evolution in that environment, that data is displayed on the right.
